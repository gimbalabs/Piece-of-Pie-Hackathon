## 💬 Feedback Loop - Feedback Pie

Reminder: feedback sessions are part of the hackathon itself — not something extra you do after the project is finished.

You do not need a polished app or completed demo to ask for feedback.

In fact, early conversations are often the most valuable.

A good feedback session can be as simple as:

- showing screenshots

- explaining the idea

- discussing blockers

- testing onboarding

- asking someone what confused them

- observing how they react to the story of the project

If you are unsure how to begin:

1. Find another builder

2. Schedule a short call

3. Record the session

4. Ask honest questions

5. Document the session with relevent information and video link

The goal is not approval. The goal is learning faster together. Users and developers party together.

## 🔧 Weekly Focus: Expansion

Expansion is a natural next step after deep work and focused iteration.

Builders often move through cycles:  
focus → build → test → share → refine → focus again.

Last week emphasized deep work, narrowing attention, and improving the core of the project. This week is about widening the circle and letting more people see what you are building.

Expansion means:

- sharing your work publicly
- telling the story of your product
- attracting users and testers
- improving onboarding
- increasing visibility and engagement

A project grows when people can understand it, explain it, and share it with others.

If an idea cannot yet be clearly described, it becomes difficult for users to connect with it. Storytelling is part of product development. The way you explain your project helps define the project itself.

This week, think about distribution:

- How do people discover your project?
- How do they understand its value?
- How do they become users?
- How do they invite others?

Let people see what you’re building.

Growth often begins with a clear story shared consistently.

## 🍰 Reminder

Projects grow the same way many natural systems grow:  
through cycles of focus and expansion.

There are times to narrow attention, refine the core, and improve quietly.  
And there are times to share, distribute, connect, and invite others in.

Both are necessary.

This week, let people see what you’re building.

A project hidden from the world cannot gather momentum.
