## 💬 Feedback Loop

Feedback is why we build in public.  It's about learning what users want, validating before over-building, and getting out of the building. 

- *The Mom Test* — Rob Fitzpatrick
- *The Minimalist Entrepreneur* — Sahil Lavingia (Gumroad)
- Noah Kagan's *Million Dollar Weekend*
- Y Combinator's "How to Talk to Customers"
- Steve Blank's Customer Development framework

**One takeaway:** Don't ask users if your idea is good. Ask about their problems, their current behavior, and what they've already tried. **The goal of feedback isn't approval—it's learning what to build next.**

---

## 🔧 Focus for Next Week: Real Users

Feedback from other builders is valuable. Feedback from real users is essential.

At some point, every builder must leave the comfort of planning, coding, and discussing ideas and put something in front of the people they hope to serve.

This week, challenge yourself to:

- talk with potential users
- show your work
- ask questions 
- observe how people actually respond

Look for signals of value: a user who returns, a problem that resonates, a commitment to try your product, or even a first transaction.

Remember: the goal is not to convince people your idea is good. The goal is to discover whether you're solving a real problem for real people.

Because in the end, demos are interesting. Real users are what count.
