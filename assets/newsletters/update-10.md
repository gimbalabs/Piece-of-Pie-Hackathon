## 🔧Weekly Focus:

Building is important. Proving that you built something is equally important.

As builders, we make assumptions all the time: assumptions about users, about problems, about solutions, and about the value we create. Proof is how we test those assumptions against reality.

Proof comes in many forms:

- weekly updates
- commits and pull requests
- user conversations
- feedback sessions
- screenshots and demos
- metrics and usage data
- testimonials and transactions

The key is not to collect data for its own sake. Good evidence helps answer a question or validate an assumption. A hundred vanity metrics are less valuable than one meaningful signal that shows a real user found value in what you built.

Documentation is a powerful tool in this process.

It helps you remember where you've been, explain your work to others, and pick up where you left off when life pulls you away from a project. It creates a record of progress that can be shared, challenged, improved, and built upon.

In many ways, builders are gathering evidence much like a lawyer builds a case. Claims are interesting. Proof is convincing.

This is why the Piece of Pie Hackathon asks builders to document their work through weekly updates, GitHub activity, feedback sessions, and final presentations.

If you didn't document it, it didn't happen.
