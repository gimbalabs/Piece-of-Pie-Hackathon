## 🔧 Weekly Focus: Preparing Your Final Presentation

The finish line is HERE!

Over the next 9 days, prepare your final presentation. The goal is not to create a polished slide deck or an elaborate pitch. (Save the polished pitch deck for Draper Dragon.) The goal is simply to show what you built.

The Piece of Pie [presentation format is provided as a guide](https://github.com/gimbalabs/Piece-of-Pie-Hackathon/blob/main/assets/templates/final-presentation-template.md)—not a template you must follow exactly. Feel free to organize your presentation in whatever way best tells the story of your project.

To qualify, your presentation should include these essential parts:

- A live demonstration video of your product

- A link to the deployed public product

- A link to the official public repository

- Links to your required 12 weekly on X.com

- Your builder identification information

Everything else is optional.

If screenshots, user feedback, metrics, testimonials, or lessons learned help explain your project, include them. If they don't, leave them out.

As you prepare, ask yourself one simple question:

**If someone saw my presentation for the first time, would they understand what I built and why it matters?**

That's the goal.

Keep it clear,  honest, and  simple.

The Piece of Pie Hackathon was designed to **encourage builders, not paperwork.** Your presentation should reflect the work you've already done—not create unnecessary new work.

Finish strong, and let your product speak for itself.

## Reminder

One of the best things about a hackathon isn't the software.

It's the people you meet along the way.

Take a moment to look back at the builders who gave you feedback, answered a question, inspired an idea, or simply encouraged you to keep going.

Before the season ends, thank someone who helped you - and let them know why.

Better yet, invite them to one last feedback session.
