# FAQ

## What kind of project can qualify?

A qualifying Builder project must be a user-facing software product with a clear payment gate.

Examples may include:

- SaaS products
- web applications
- web3 applications
- tools with clear user value

Projects that only run locally do not qualify.

## Can I work alone?

Yes. Solo and team projects are both allowed.

## Can I join more than one project?

No. Each participant may only join one project.

## Does every team member need to be named?

Yes. Every contributor must be explicitly listed during registration.

## Can we use a private repository?

No. The official repository must be public and remain public until final payouts are complete.

## Does the repository need to start empty?

Yes. The official repository must start empty at the beginning of the hackathon.

## What happens if I miss a weekly tweet?

Missing a required weekly tweet is disqualifying for Builder qualification.

## What must a weekly tweet include?

Each required tweet must:

- show progress
- include `#gimbalabs`
- include `#pieceofpie`
- include `#hackathon`
- mention `@gimbalabs`

## Do I need a deployed product?

For Builder qualification, yes. By the end of the event, the product must be publicly accessible on its intended platform.  For qualifying for the Feedback pie, you don't need to build and deploy a product - only volunteer and be accepted on the discord channel. 

## What counts for the Real User Pie?

You must first qualify through the Builder Pie or Cardano Pie requirements, then show at least one real paying user, payment evidence, and a public customer acquisition story.

## What is the Cardano Pie?

The Cardano Pie is a 20,000 ADA reward pool sponsored by the Cardano Foundation for qualifying projects that build real functionality on Cardano mainnet.

## What does a Cardano Pie project need to do?

A Cardano Pie project must satisfy every Builder Pie requirement, include payment gating, and deliver real functionality on Cardano mainnet.

## Can a Cardano project also receive the Builder Pie?

No. For qualifying Cardano mainnet projects, the Cardano Pie replaces the Builder Pie.

## Who gets to pitch to Draper Dragon?

Qualified Cardano Pie projects get an opportunity to pitch to the Draper Dragon VC Fund.

## What counts as payment evidence?

Any verifiable evidence that clearly shows a real customer paid for your product can count.

Examples include:

- payment receipts
- paid invoices
- billing records
- bank transfer records
- app store or platform payout records
- blockchain transaction records with enough supporting context

## Can family and friends count as paying users?

No. Family and friends do not count as paying users.

## Do I need to be a Builder to participate in the Feedback Pie?

No. You may participate in the Feedback track even without a builder project.

## How do feedback sessions start?

Join the Gimbalabs Discord, complete server verification, and use the official Piece of Pie feedback forum (#piece-of-pie-feedback-requests).

Flow:

- a builder posts a feedback request
- a feedback giver volunteers in the thread
- the builder team may accept or refuse the volunteer before the recorded session starts
- the session must be live and recorded to count

Use the general Piece of Pie discussion channel for broader questions and discussion.

## What is the recommend tool to record a feedback session?

Any comparable service is also acceptable if it creates a shareable recording that can be verified.

Zoom is recommended because the free account allows for recorded sessions up to 40 min.  

The project owner requesting feedback may create the Zoom room, host the live feedback session, record it, and share the recording with the feedback giver for evidence.

A free Zoom account is enough if the host uses Zoom's computer recording option and then uploads or shares the recording file. Cloud recording may require a paid Zoom account. Other comparable services are also acceptable if they create a shareable recording that can be verified.

## How can I raise questions or issues about the hackathon itself?

If a rule, edge case, or interpretation is unclear, [open an issue in this repository](https://github.com/gimbalabs/Piece-of-Pie-Hackathon/issues).

When opening an issue:

- use a clear title
- describe the question in plain language
- link to the relevant doc section if possible
- include a concrete example or scenario
- keep one issue focused on one question

Admins may use those issues to publish clarifications or record formal rulings later.
